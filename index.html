  • Stealth III whistles are designed to look and feel very similar to traditional mass produced whistles, but are made to very strict tolerances and thoroughly tested before shipment.

Options

  • Tubes with the same bore diameter, regardless of key, will fit the same size mouthpiece. This makes it possible to have multi-tube sets that share a single mouthpiece, which is more economical than having complete whistles in each key. Please note that only tubes that have the same bore diameter are interchangeable with a shared mouthpiece, as there are both Stealth and Journeyman tubes with the same tube diameter that will not fit the same mouthpiece. Journeyman tubes are turned down as thin as Stealth tubes on the end that inserts into the mouthpiece, so disregard tube diameter when choosing multi-tube sets.
  • For an additional $5.00 charge, left thumb holes may be added to any tube to allow a perfectly in tune flattened 7th note in both octaves without the need for cross-fingering.

  • Is there any difference in tone between brass/aluminum/silver tubes?
    • The choice of metal imparts very little difference in tone with high whistle tubes. The difference in tone only becomes discernible in low whistles, and the deeper resonance of aluminum tubes is ideally suited for low keys. Silver whistles sound a bit brighter than brass, but the difference in tone is subtle.

  • Does the added thickness of the aluminum Journeyman tubes change the playing characteristics of the whistles?
    • The finger holes on the thicker Journeyman tubes are deeper, which necessitates making them a bit larger in diameter. The deeper finger holes also add a bit of tuning stability that makes it more difficult to blow notes sharp. This is especially useful for the cross-fingered flattened 7th note if you prefer to use the easier OXX OOO fingering instead of the traditional OXX XOX forked fingering.

  • What is the difference between Trad/Session/Wide Bore whistles?
    • The Trad whistles have narrow bore tubes. Narrow bore whistles have a bit less volume and an easier second octave compared to larger bores. Most mass-produced whistles have narrow bores, and Trad whistle tubes are the Imperial equivalent of the Metric size tubes used by most manufacturers of mass-produced whistles. Trad whistles are usually the best choice for players who are already used to the playing characteristics of Generations, Feadogs, and other such mass-produced whistles.
    • The Session whistles have tubes that are just a bit larger than Trad whistles. They have just a bit more volume than the Trad whistles, which makes them better suited for noisier environments like sessions.
    • The Wide Bore D and the D+ have the same bore size as a Trad C, and require more push in the second octave. Both of these whistles are quite loud, and are best suited for outdoor venues or larger sessions.
    • Each time the bore size is increased for a particular key, the resulting whistle is a bit louder, the second octave requires a bit more push, and some of the "sweetness" is lost from the tone.

  • Do you include a pouch or case with your whistles?
    • Sorry, I don't. I have long recommended the vinyl pouches sold by Susato if you're looking for something cheap that provides adequate protection. If you'd rather have something nicer looking and sturdier, then I recommend Tony's Leather Creations. Tony's leather cases provide much better protection than any thin vinyl pouch ever could.
